Self-Induction Phenomenon

A coil (inductor) of inductance \(L\) and internal resistance \(r\) opposes any variation of the current \(i\) flowing through it. The induced EMF is given by \(e = -L \frac{di}{dt}\). In a circuit with a DC source \(E\) and a resistor \(R\), the establishment of current follows the law: \(i(t) = \frac{E}{R+r}(1 - e^{-t/\tau})\).
